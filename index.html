Utility Classes

All utility classes follow a familiar naming scheme. A shorthand reference to the property, such as mb for margin-bottom, a hyphen, then a number 0 through 9 which represents the size in rem units.

The values that 1-9 correspond with are

  1. 0.5rem
  2. 0.75rem
  3. 1rem
  4. 1.5rem
  5. 2rem
  6. 3rem
  7. 4rem
  8. 5rem
  9. 10rem

Certain utility classes have more than just 0-9, such as .m-auto which applies a margin of auto.

In addition, forget.css uses a base 10 system, meaning 1rem is equal to 10px.

Read more about the utility classes on their respective pages.
